# Carnaval 2026 Demand Forecast Analysis

## Overview
This project focuses on forecasting attendance and ticket demand for a large-scale winter event inspired by Carnaval de Québec.

The objective is to understand how different factors such as day of week, weather conditions, and marketing intensity influence attendance levels and overall demand.

## Business Objective
Support decision-making for event planning and marketing investment by answering:

- Which days generate the highest attendance?
- How does weather impact demand?
- What is the effect of marketing intensity on ticket sales?
- How can attendance be maximized during weaker periods?

## Dataset
The dataset simulates daily event conditions during Carnaval 2026.

It includes:
- Date
- Day of week
- Temperature
- Weather conditions (snow, clear, etc.)
- Marketing intensity
- Estimated attendance
- Ticket sales

## Key Metrics
- Daily attendance
- Ticket sales
- Demand trends over time
- Impact of external factors (weather, marketing)

## Key Insights
- Weekends generate significantly higher attendance than weekdays
- Extreme cold negatively impacts attendance
- Moderate snowfall does not reduce attendance significantly
- Higher marketing intensity correlates with increased ticket sales

## Sample Results

Based on the dataset:

- Peak attendance observed during weekends
- Strong correlation between marketing intensity and ticket sales
- Attendance drops during extreme weather conditions

## Recommendations
- Increase marketing efforts before and during weekends
- Adapt programming during extreme cold periods to maintain attendance
- Use targeted campaigns during weaker weekdays
- Align major attractions with peak demand days
